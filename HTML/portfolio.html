Chassis

Our robot uses a frame with GoBilda parts, including motors, channels, brackets, beams, and more. It runs on mecanum wheels for a stronger base, easier driving during TeleOp, more consistency, and better support for odometry. It also provides us with a narrower base, which lets the robot navigate around the drive barrier. A raised flywheel is positioned behind the arm in the front of the robot to spin the carousel wheel and deliver ducks during TeleOp. Recently, we attached compliant wheels to the sides of the robot to prevent it from crashing into the wall.



Our new robot chassis.

Intake

Our intake is one of the most innovative parts on our robot. We wanted a quick and efficient intake that could pick up all sorts of cargo quickly. In order to achieve this, we needed to connect a 3D-printed part to the arm. We used Fusion 360 for all of our 3D designs. Finalizing the intake took tons of trial and error, since the 3D printed part caused problems with durability and functionality. Now, our intake system follows these steps:

  • 1. Intake the cargo by spinning the intake wheels and storing it in the 3D printed part
  • 2. Raise the arm to the desired level of the shipping hub
  • 3. Spin the intake wheels in the opposite direction to drop the cargo

Our intake 3D design (left) and our actual intake (right)

Arm

The arm of our robot works harmoniously with the intake system. Before the makeover, our robot used a simple four-bar arm. Now, we replaced it with an extendable boom in order to fit the size limit and reach efficiently for all levels of the shipping hub. It is responsible for lifting the intake system.

Our extendable robot arm

Measuring Tape System

Recently, we added a 3D-printed measuring tape system controlled by a servo and two wheels. This system saves us valuable time in the Endgame phase, as we will be using it to drop the team shipping element onto the shipping hub without stopping the robot. To achieve this, we will use a camera to detect the shipping hub and rotate the measuring tape system appropriately.
